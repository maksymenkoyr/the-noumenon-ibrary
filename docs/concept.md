# Concept

## The premise

The library claims to contain all possible ideas **within the horizon of human writing** — vast but honest about its horizon. Not all possible character strings (Borges). Not all human knowledge outside the written record. Everything that has been thought, known, and imagined *within* what has been written and digitized.

The shape of the library reflects the shape of machine cognition. That horizon is part of the artwork, not a flaw to hide.

Completeness is experiential, not mathematical. High temperature + randomization instructions gets close enough to "all possible human ideas" for the premise to hold.

---

## The noumenon

Kant's *noumenon* is the thing-in-itself: the object as it exists independently of perception, forever beyond full apprehension. We only ever encounter phenomena — things as they appear to our senses and cognition.

The library takes that idea literally. Pages exist beyond the reach of any single reading. The library pre-exists in principle; it materializes as people walk it. No visitor can ever see the whole.

---

## The Library of Babel

Borges' library contains all possible books — every permutation of every character. Most are noise. A few, by chance, are coherent. The horror and the wonder are the same thing.

This library inverts the ratio: every page is coherent-but-strange, dreamlike, *meant* — not stumbled upon by chance but crystallized from latent space.

---

## Shared and canonical

One library, shared across all users. Not per-user sandboxes. Pages crystallize as the library is explored; **being the first visitor to an address is an event**.

The library pre-exists in principle. It materializes as people walk it.

---

## The uncanny target zone

*Coherent but hollow* is the primary failure mode — readable, forgettable, empty.

The target is the middle of a journey from coherence toward strangeness. The library should feel like it means something you cannot quite name. Pages that produce a pause. Not noise, not prose — something between.

Entropy levers (see [Generation](./generation.md)) are the mechanism for reaching and maintaining that zone over time.
